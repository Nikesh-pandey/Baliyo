Reso in Toronto

Sure! "Reso" in Toronto typically refers to the PATH, an extensive network of underground pedestrian walkways beneath the city's downtown area. It's like an underground city, connecting office buildings, shopping centers, transit stations, and other landmarks. The PATH is especially popular during the winter because it offers a warm and sheltered way to get around without having to go outside in the cold weather. It's a convenient and bustling place, filled with shops, restaurants, and services, making it a hub of activity for both locals and visitors alike.

FAQ's About RESO

RESO refers to the PATH, an extensive network of underground pedestrian walkways beneath downtown Toronto.

The PATH spans over 30 kilometers (19 miles) and connects various office buildings, shopping centers, transit stations, and tourist attractions.

The origin of the term "RESO" isn't entirely clear, but it's a colloquial name often used by locals to refer to the PATH.
